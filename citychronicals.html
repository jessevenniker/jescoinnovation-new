Situation

Thousands of cities.
Zero memorable apps.

Most city guides do the same thing: lists, stars, directions. Useful, but flat. They answer the question "where" without ever touching "why".

CityChronicles starts from a different premise. What if discovering a city felt like following a good story — with rhythm, surprise, and moments worth sharing?

Approach

Routes as narratives.
Exploration as entertainment.

The design is built around structured discovery: themed routes, local quizzes, and story-driven moments that turn a neighbourhood walk into something you actually remember. Not a map. A guide with a point of view.

Every design decision serves one principle: the city should feel curated, not overwhelming. Guided, not rigid. The platform earns attention by making exploration genuinely enjoyable.

Result

A new standard
for city storytelling.

CityChronicles doesn't compete with a map — it complements it. A layer of meaning on top of a city that makes visitors curious, and keeps locals engaged.

The design reflects the platform itself: sharp, playful, and unmistakably intentional. A product that feels as good to look at as it is to use.
