Situation

Great word of mouth.
Invisible online.

The operator had been running day sails and sunset cruises out of Bonaire for years. Repeat guests, strong personal recommendations, a genuinely beautiful experience on the water. But when new visitors searched before arriving on the island, nothing appeared.

The existing web presence was minimal — an outdated booking platform page that stripped every trace of atmosphere from the experience. Nothing about it communicated the quality of being out on open water with someone who knows the reef better than anyone. Competitors with inferior offerings were getting found first.

Approach

Atmosphere first.
Function second.

Most sailing tour websites look the same: a stock photo, a price list, a booking widget. We went the other direction. The design leads with the experience itself — the quality of light on the water at dusk, the quietness of Bonaire's coastline away from the dive boats, the personal nature of a small-vessel charter.

Enquiries flow through WhatsApp. The skipper responds personally. The site was built to reflect that relationship from the first scroll — this isn't a transaction, it's a conversation with someone who does this for the love of it. No booking engines, no mandatory fields, no friction between a visitor's interest and a direct reply.

Result

A site that earns
its own guests.

Within the first weeks of launch, direct enquiries began arriving from visitors who had found the site through Google before travelling to Bonaire. For the first time, the website was functioning as an actual sales channel — reaching guests who hadn't heard about the operator through word of mouth.

The WhatsApp flow kept the booking process human. Higher conversion than a form, because guests felt they were already in contact with a real person before they'd even confirmed a date. The same quality the operator offers on the water, reflected from the first click.
